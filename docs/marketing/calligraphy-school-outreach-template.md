# Japanese Calligraphy School Outreach Template

This template is designed for outreach to traditional 書道 (shodo) schools in Japan. It uses a combination of Japanese and English, acknowledging limited Japanese ability while maintaining proper business etiquette.

---

## Email Template

**Subject:** 書道教室とのパートナーシップのご相談 / Partnership Inquiry from MichiKanji

---

[School Name] 御中

突然のご連絡失礼いたします。

My name is [Your Name], and I am the founder of MichiKanji (michikanji.com), an online kanji learning platform.

まず、私の日本語が不十分であることをお詫び申し上げます。このメールは日本語と英語を混ぜて書かせていただきます。
*(First, I apologize that my Japanese is limited. I am writing this email in a mix of Japanese and English.)*

---

**ご連絡の理由 / Why I Am Reaching Out:**

MichiKanji serves thousands of students worldwide who are actively studying kanji and Japanese. Many of our users express interest in deepening their practice through traditional 書道 (shodo).

I discovered [School Name] through my research and was impressed by [specific detail about their school].

**ご提案 / Proposal:**

I would like to discuss a potential partnership:

- **Exposure to motivated learners**: Our website traffic includes students specifically interested in kanji mastery
- **Featured listing**: We could introduce your school to our audience as a recommended resource for serious learners
- **Mutual benefit**: Your school gains visibility among dedicated students; our users discover authentic shodo instruction

---

**次のステップ / Next Steps:**

お時間をいただければ、短いオンラインミーティングまたはメールでのやり取りで詳細をご相談できれば幸いです。

*(If you have time, I would be grateful to discuss details through a brief online meeting or email exchange.)*

ご検討いただけますと幸いです。
*(I would appreciate your consideration.)*

---

お忙しいところ恐れ入りますが、ご返信いただければ幸いです。

敬具

[Your Name]
MichiKanji / ミチ漢字
[Your Email]
[Website URL]

## Cultural Notes

| Element | Guidance |
|---------|----------|
| **御中 (onchū)** | Use for addressing organizations; use 様 (sama) if writing to a specific person by name |
| **突然のご連絡失礼いたします** | Standard polite opener for cold emails ("Please excuse my sudden contact") |
| **Apology for language** | Being upfront about your Japanese level is respectful and sets expectations |
| **敬具 (keigu)** | Formal closing equivalent to "Sincerely" |
| **Timing** | Avoid sending during O-Bon (mid-August) or year-end/New Year periods |

## Follow-Up Schedule

- **First email**: Initial outreach
- **Wait 7-10 days**: Japanese business culture often requires longer response times
- **Second email**: Brief, polite follow-up
- **Wait 2 weeks**: Give additional time
- **After 2 attempts**: Move to next contact unless they express interest
